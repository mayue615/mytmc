Club Mission:To provide a mutually supportive and positive learning environment in which every individual member has the opportunity to develop oral communication and leadership skills, which in turn foster self-confidence and personal growth.
Three Etiquettes:
1) Before and after your speech, shake hands with the person inviting you
2) No walk in/out of the room during the meeting, shift your cell phone into slient mode
3)To be a member, you should: Attend our meeting 3 times and deliver ice breaker speech.
Key links in our meeting:
Table topic: Every member or guest has a chance to deliver a unprepared speech within 1'-2'.
Prepared speech: Deliver prepared speech following the manuel, members only except ice breaker.
Evaluation: Feedbacks will be given to make improvements possible.
Speech Objectives:
C1:Ice Breaker Begin public speaking before audiences.
C2:Organize your speech Select an appropriate outline which allows listeners to easily follow.
C3:Get to the point Select a speech topic and determine its general specific purposes.
C4:How to say it Select the right words and sentence structure to communicate your ideas clearly.
C5:Your body speaks Use stance, movement ,gestures ,facial expressions&eye contacts.
C6:Vocal Variety Use voice volume, pitch, rate&quality to reflect&add Meaning and interest.
C7:Research Your Topic Collect information about your topic from numerous sources.
C8:Get comfortable with Visual Aids Select visual aids appropriate for your message&listeners.
C9:Persuade with Power Persuade listeners to adopt your viewpoint or ideas or to take actions.
C10:Inspire your audience Inspire by appealing to noble motives&challenging listeners to achieve.
